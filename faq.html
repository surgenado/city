What is a Surgenado?

  1. Surgenado is a method for elevating the unit price of services rendered in price multipler governed marketplaces without impacting the quality of service or cumulative contractor earnings.
  2. The Surgenado method is constructed on two key assumptions.
    1. Price multipliers in price multiplier governed marketplaces are weighted towards ensuring supply by deincentivizing demand and therefore are overly sensitive to reductions in supply.
    2. In maturing high-growth marketplaces where growth was sustained through aggressive financial incentives, there is an exploitable gap between the base price and the price at which price aversion negatively impacts demand.
  3. Surgenado.com/{{ site.title }} is the apparatus for implementing the Surgenado method in the {{ site.title }} market
  4. Contractor unavailabilty is initially scheduled in 30 minute blocks staggered at 15 minute intervals.
    As the Surgenado gains momentum, website analytics are used to analyze and then shape participation patterns to increase impact.
  5. Surgenado.com functions as an organizational tool until a critical momentum is achieved when it shifts into an operational mode.
